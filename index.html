Literature Survey

Figure 2. Ornamental fish farming

“The ornamental fish industry in Sri Lanka was first an area of unidentified export potential when fish lovers used to import their favorite fish varieties back in the 1930s.”.Ornamental fish farming is the practice of breeding and raising colorful fish varieties of various species for commercial purposes. Today, smart aquaculture represents a key trend towards sustainable development within the fish farming industry, leveraging intelligence and automation. The integration of advanced intelligent technologies has delivered substantial advantages across various sectors, including aquaculture. These technologies significantly decrease the need for manual labor, boost production efficiency, and promote environmental sustainability.

Despite the ornamental fish industry’s remarkable growth, it faces several challenges that threaten its sustainability and productivity, especially in the areas of feeding practices, water quality management and disease control. Traditional methods in these areas often result in inefficiencies, such as overfeeding, inadequate nutrient supply, and inconsistent water quality monitoring, resulting in increased mortality and disease outbreaks, thus underscoring the urgent need for innovative solutions.The domain of aquaculture has witnessed a notable progression in the incorporation of Internet of Things (IoT) technology for the purpose of monitoring and regulating water quality parameters in fish farming settings. To clarify the effectiveness and significance of IoT-based systems in managing water parameters in aquaculture settings, this literature review summarizes important data from pertinent studies.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Automated Feeding and Water Quality Monitoring

There are limited solutions designed specifically for automated feeding and real-time water quality monitoring in ornamental fish farming. Current technologies do not adapt feeding schedules based on real-time data, and existing water quality systems lack the accuracy and reliability needed for small-scale operations.

Disease Detection and Management

There is a scarcity of advanced disease management systems for ornamental fish that can detect and diagnose diseases early using image processing and machine learning techniques. Existing methods are predominantly manual and lack real-time monitoring and predictive capabilities.

Predictive Analytics and Real-time Communication

The application of predictive analytics and machine learning in ornamental fish farming is minimal. Additionally, effective real-time communication systems for data sharing between farmers, researchers, and extension services are underdeveloped, which is crucial for timely decision-making and improved farm management.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

How to optimize feeding, water quality monitoring, and disease management in ornamental fish farming using IoT technology?

Ornamental fish farming faces significant challenges in maintaining optimal water quality, providing precise feeding, and managing diseases effectively. Traditional methods are manual, labor-intensive, and often lack real-time data, leading to inefficiencies and potential losses. The unique conditions of diverse and confined ponds used for ornamental fish farming further complicate these tasks. To improve the efficiency and sustainability of ornamental fish farming, there is a need for a smart, integrated solution that leverages IoT technology.


Figure 3. Diseased fish

Proposed Solution

Our IoT-based system aims to revolutionize ornamental fish farming by providing a comprehensive solution that integrates automated feeding, real-time water quality monitoring, and advanced disease management. The system uses ESP32 microcontrollers combined with various sensors and machine learning algorithms to offer precise and adaptive control over the farming environment. The system includes an automated feeding mechanism that adjusts feeding schedules based on real-time temperature collected from DHT11 sensor. This ensures that the fish receive the right amount of food at optimal times, enhancing their growth and health. Real-time monitoring of critical water parameters such as pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and ammonia level are achieved through advanced sensors. The data is processed using predictive analytics to maintain water quality within optimal ranges, reducing the risk of disease and improving fish welfare. The system employs image processing and machine learning techniques to detect and diagnose fish diseases early. Using cameras and deep learning models, it can identify symptoms and provide alerts for timely intervention. This proactive approach helps prevent the spread of diseases and reduces mortality rates. The sensors are designed for durability and reliability, with self-cleaning mechanisms to ensure long-term functionality. The system is tailored to the unique conditions of ornamental fishponds, providing accurate and consistent data. A robust communication infrastructure allows for real-time data sharing between farmers, researchers, and extension services. This facilitates informed decision-making and enables timely interventions. Notifications and alerts are sent to users' mobile devices, ensuring they are always aware of the farm's status. By addressing these critical areas, our IoT-based solution aims to enhance the efficiency, productivity, and sustainability of ornamental fish farming. This integrated approach not only improves farm management but also contributes to the overall health and well-being of ornamental fish./P>

Research Objectives

Automated Feeding Optimization

The first objective is to develop an automated feeding system that adjusts feeding schedules based on real-time data to optimize nutrition and growth for ornamental fish. This system will ensure that the fish receive the appropriate amount of food at the right times, enhancing their health and minimizing waste.

Real-time Water Quality Monitoring

The second objective is to implement a real-time water quality monitoring system that accurately measures and maintains critical parameters such as pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and ammonia levels. This system will use predictive analytics to ensure optimal water conditions, reducing the risk of disease and improving overall fish welfare.

Advanced Disease Detection and Management

The third objective is to create an advanced disease management system that utilizes image processing and machine learning techniques to detect and diagnose fish diseases early. This system will provide timely alerts and recommendations for intervention, helping to prevent the spread of diseases and reduce mortality rates.

Real-time Data Communication and Integration

The fourth objective is to establish a robust communication infrastructure that facilitates real-time data sharing among farmers, researchers, and extension services. This system will enhance decision-making by providing mobile notifications and alerts, ensuring that stakeholders are always informed about the farm's status and any necessary actions to be taken.

Methodology

Figure 4. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed IoT-based system for ornamental fish farming consists of four main components:

  1. Automated Feeding System
  2. Real-time Water Quality Monitoring
  3. Advanced Disease Detection and Management
  4. Real-time Data Communication and Integration

The automated feeding system uses sensors to gather real-time data on environmental conditions and fish behavior. This data is processed to adjust feeding schedules, ensuring optimal nutrition and growth. The system leverages machine learning algorithms to continuously improve feeding efficiency.The water quality monitoring system employs advanced sensors to measure critical parameters such as pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and ammonia levels. Data is transmitted to an ESP32 microcontroller, which processes the information using predictive analytics to maintain optimal water conditions. The system provides real-time alerts if any parameter deviates from the ideal range.The disease detection system uses cameras to capture images of the fish. These images are sent to a backend server where deep learning models, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), are deployed for disease identification. The system can detect symptoms early and determine the severity of diseases, providing timely alerts and recommendations for intervention.The system includes a robust communication infrastructure to facilitate real-time data sharing among farmers. Data from sensors and disease detection models is integrated and visualized on a dashboard, accessible via mobile devices.

The overall system provides a smart, integrated approach to managing ornamental fish farming, improving efficiency, productivity, and fish health. The real-time monitoring and automated control mechanisms ensure that all critical parameters are maintained within optimal ranges, reducing the risk of disease and enhancing the overall sustainability of the farming operations.
